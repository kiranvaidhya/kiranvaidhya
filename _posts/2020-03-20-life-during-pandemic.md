# Life in a pandemic

It has been a terrible few days since [WHO officially classified COVID-19 as a pandemic](https://www.who.int/dg/speeches/detail/who-director-general-s-opening-remarks-at-the-media-briefing-on-covid-19---11-march-2020). 
I have no idea how I survived the onslaught of panic. Fear is contagious, even more so than the actual virus.
I had to deactivate my Twitter account and delete the app from my phone to stop myself from scrolling over toxic newsfeeds.
That's the downside of having your Twitter newsfeed optimized towards a scientific community. 
Everyone's urging panic to stem the exponential growth, with some even going to the extent of having conversations about collapse of civilizations.
Needless to say, I decided to cut down on some of the negativity in my life.

It's been a week since I started working from home. I seem to have a routine now. I cook my own food everyday. I started working out today.
I have stocked up enough food for a couple of weeks. I wouldn't be surprised if the Netherlands imposes martial law to stop people from going out.
STAY HOME AND WASH YOUR HANDS! You don't have to go out and be irresponsible.

Italy is the worst affected European country. The scenes are horrible wth military trucks carrying coffins of dead people.
At the time of writing, Italy appears to be two months behind Wuhan. The situation was equally dire for Wuhan in January 2020 when they decided to build two hospitals from scratch in the space of a week.
Right now, in March 2020, China has reported zero new cases of COVID-19. It's a remarkable achievement.
Although, I still hold suspicions over the authenticity of their data. It could very well be a political message sent across to the world to pursue authoritarian governments over democracy.
Nevertheless, I sincerely wish China shares its data with European countries to control the pandemic in the most effective manner.

If things go well, I'd expect Europe to recover from the pandemic in the next 3-4 months. Fingers crossed.
